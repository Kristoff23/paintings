Heroes: José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda

Date of birth and death: June 19, 1861 - December 30, 1896

José Rizal was a writer, scholar, and patriot who played a significant role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule. His two best-known novels, "Noli me tangere" and "El filibusterismo," exposed the excesses and injustices of Spanish colonial rule and instilled a sense of resistance and national identity in the Filipino people. He is considered a national hero in the Philippines.

Heroes: Andrés Bonifacio y de Castro

Date of birth and death: November 30, 1863 - May 10, 1897

Andrés Bonifacio y de Castro, nicknamed "the great plebeian" and the "father of the Philippine Revolution," was a revolutionary and nationalist from the Philippines who established the Katipunan, a clandestine group tasked with toppling Spanish colonial rule. Although formal ceremonies do not honor Bonifacio as a national hero, he is well known for his contributions to the fight for Filipino independence.

Heroes: Emilio Aguinaldo y Famy

Date of birth and death: March 22, 1869 - February 6, 1964

Emilio Aguinaldo, a prominent participant in the Philippine independence movement, led his country to independence from Spanish rule in 1898 and went on to serve as the republic's first president.

Heroes: Marcelo Hilario del Pilar y Gatmaitán

Date of birth and death: September 11, 1917 - September 28, 1989

Marcelo Hilario del Pilar y Gatmaitan, a Filipino writer, lawyer, and revolutionary propagandist better known by his stage name Plaridel, was renowned for his organizational skills and satirical writings. He played a significant role in the Spanish Reform Movement as well. Because of his editorials and essays that appeared in La Solidaridad, he is recognized as the "Father of Philippine Journalism" and is the true brains behind the Katipunan.

Heroes: Ferdinand Emmanuel Edralin Marcos Sr.

Date of birth and death: September 11, 1917 - September 28, 1989

Marcos Ferdinand was a politician and lawyer from the Philippines who led the nation from 1965 to 1986. He is best known for establishing an authoritarian regime marked by corruption and the suppression of opposition.
