José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda

Born in : June 19, 1861

José Rizal was a writer, scholar, and patriot who played a significant role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule. His two best-known novels, "Noli me tangere" and "El filibusterismo," exposed the excesses and injustices of Spanish colonial rule and instilled a sense of resistance and national identity in the Filipino people. He is considered a national hero in the Philippines.

Andrés Bonifacio y de Castro

Born in : November 30, 1863

Andrés Bonifacio y de Castro, nicknamed "the great plebeian" and the "father of the Philippine Revolution," was a revolutionary and nationalist from the Philippines who established the Katipunan, a clandestine group tasked with toppling Spanish colonial rule. Although formal ceremonies do not honor Bonifacio as a national hero, he is well known for his contributions to the fight for Filipino independence.

Emilio Aguinaldo y Famy

Born in : March 22, 1869

Emilio Aguinaldo, a prominent participant in the Philippine independence movement, led his country to independence from Spanish rule in 1898 and went on to serve as the republic's first president.
